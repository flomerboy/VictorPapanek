Victor Papanek was a notable designer, educator, and futurist.
These are some of the things he said.

"There are professions more harmful than industrial design but only a very few of them."

"... any attempt to separate design, to make it a thing-by-itself, works counter to the inherent value of design as the primary underlying matrix of life."

"In an environment that is screwed up visually, physically, and chemically, the best and simplest thing that architects, industrial designers, planners, etc. could do for humanity would be to stop working entirely"

"I am just hoping for product statements aesthetically acceptible enough not to conjure up visions of a bread box raped by a cadillac in heat."

on Bauhaus furniture...
"... the first reaction on encountering such a table is to lie down on it and have your appendix extracted"

"In a dramatically changing world society that is (tremblingly) afraid of change and that educates its young into ever-narrowing areas of specialization, the integrated, comprehensive, anticipatory designer is a dedicated synthesist"

"The road to a better future is a two-way street"

Design for Human Scale, Van Nostrand Reinhold company, New York, 1983. Print.
Design for the Real World: Human Ecology and Social Change, Victor Papanek, Pantheon Books, New York, 1971. Print.
